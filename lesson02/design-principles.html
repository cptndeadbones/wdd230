Design Principles

Contrast

Company: Krai by Zak Steel-Eklund

Website: Dribble
https://dribbble.com/shots/5298824-K-R-A-I/attachments/1149295

On the website Dribble, the designer Zak Steele-Eklund posted a sample of a website he had built. This site Krai beautifully showcases many design principles such as contrast, whitespace, visual hierarchy and many others. I particularly liked this site because of the over all aesthetic and consistancy that gives users confidence as they browes the site.

One of the greastest examples of contrast on the site is the use of purple and its complementary colors yellow and orange. There is such a natural contrast between these two colors that it does not strain the eye. These colors coupled with the soft round features just make the overall feel of the site inviting.

White space and clean design.

Company: Monolith

Website: Dribble
https://dribbble.com/Radium/projects/725683-Product

Monolith is a concept website that showcases off the idea of an A.I. personal assistant. This simple design uses a two column aproach with an image and a text content section on each row. This minimal amount of content keeps the overall feel of the site looking clean and open. By using white space the designer is able to keep the users attention on the most relevant parts of the website.

An added benefit to using white space and clean design, is that it lends it self to the clean sleek visual apperance of the product. Overall it is a very lite clean and refreshing design.

Visual Hierarchy

Company: Dex

Website: Black on White Design Trend
https://medium.muz.li/black-on-white-design-trend-497e51e108ea#.9sw0bxtzu

When it comes to visual hierarchy Dex does an amazing job. This website imediately grabs your attention with a large letter D. As your eyes are naturally drawn to the center of the page they start to find the next most important item, smaller text. Once your eyes have finished analyzing the first section they leap to the picture in the next section with the black background.

By using hierarchy in his design Dex is able to deliver a large amount of content in a very consumable way. In fact the website almost reads like a book. One of the ways Dex rewards his users is by starting each section with a letter of his name. It is not until you reach the bottom of the page that you realize what he did in spelling out or branding his product further.
